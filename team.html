Data, Content, and Product Credits

We gratefully acknowledge contributions of data and research analysis as well as images, video, and writing, and respect those who have openly shared their data and research products. These Hawaii researchers have been especially helpful.

Ruth Gates, advocate of open data (and walking the talk), supporter of people in the Gates Lab (almost all listed below) in sharing data, use here of Gates Lab website images, and Director, Hawaii Institute of Marine Biology, UH Manoa (http://www.hawaii.edu/himb).

Raphael Ritson-Williams. Use of field data, photos, video transects, and point counts derived from observations from study sites, 2014-2016 in and around Kaneohe Bay, Oahu, Hawaii. Teaching modules available (http://www.raphswall.com). Hawaii Institute of Marine Biology, UH Manoa.

Eric Hochberg, Principal Investigator, NASA CORAL project including 2016-2017 flights to gather coral data ; advising on remote sensing work in Hawaii. Bermuda Institute of Ocean Sciences (http://www.bios.edu/).

Joshua Levy, use of geotiff imagery reconstructions of high-resolution coral reef communities on patch reefs in Kaneohe Bay; part of the ground-truthing work for the NASA CORAL project. Hawaii Institute of Marine Biology, UH Manoa.

John Burns, use of 3D imagery reconstructions of coral reefs in Kaneohe Bay, Northwest Hawaiian Islands, and elsewhere. Hawaii Institute of Marine Biology, UH Manoa and UH Hilo.

Amy Eggers, use of high resolution confocal microscopy imagery of corals with (and without) their algal symbionts. Hawaii Institute of Marine Biology, UH Manoa.

Ross Cunning and Claire Hogan, sharing of not yet published observations of symbiont shuffling by corals on reefs in Kaneohe Bay. Hawaii Institute of Marine Biology, UH Manoa.

Elizabeth Lenz, graph and R code for Kaneohe Bay temperature data. Hawaii Institute of Marine Biology, UH Manoa.

Hollie Putnam; with colleagues, first published observations of symbiont shuffling by corals. Hawaii Institute of Marine Biology, UH Manoa.

Terry Hughes, James Kerry, and the ARC Centre of Excellence for Coral Reef Studies for use of videos and images of the 2016 bleaching events on the Great Barrier Reef, Australia, as well as personal communications to the science community during this time. James Cook University, Australia.

Citations
